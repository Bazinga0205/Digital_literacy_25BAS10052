The Do’s:
1)Keep your circle tight: Take a few minutes to set your accounts to private and double-check those "tagged" photos. It’s always better to be the one who decides what stays on your public profile.

2)Before hitting post, ask yourself if you’d be okay with a professor or a future boss seeing it. If it feels a bit risky, it probably is.

3)Make it work for you: Use your social media to follow people in the industry you want to join. Sharing your wins, like a big project or a certificate, is a great way to build a professional "vibe" before you even graduate.

4)Be a source of truth: We’ve all seen fake news fly around. A quick fact-check before you share something goes a long way in keeping your feed (and your reputation) credible.

5)Show your personality: Use your bio and posts to highlight the cool stuff you do outside of class—whether that’s volunteering, a hobby, or a project you’re proud of.

The Don’t

1)Keep the "wild" side offline: It might seem fun at the moment, but posting about illegal stuff or breaking campus rules is a quick way to get into hot water with the university.

2)Don’t vent-post: We all have bad days with a professor or a tough class, but keep those rants for the group chat. Complaining publicly can really come back to haunt you later.

3)Avoid the drama: It’s easy to get sucked into a heated argument in the comments, but try to stay above it. You don’t want to be linked to anything that looks like bullying or harassment.

4)Don't overshare the basics: Keep things like your student ID, your home address, or your exact "live" location to yourself. Safety first!

5)Check your handles: If you’re using a platform like LinkedIn or X for networking, make sure your username and profile picture aren't something you'd be embarrassed to explain in an interview.
